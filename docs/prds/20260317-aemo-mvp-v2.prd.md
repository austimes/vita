# VedaLang MVP Requirements v2 for AEMO 2027 IASR Support

## Purpose

This document defines the current working version of the minimum viable product requirements for the VedaLang multi-sector model intended to support AEMO’s 2027 Inputs, Assumptions and Scenarios Report (IASR) work.

It is deliberately focused on two things only:

1. the questions the model must answer
2. the minimum reference energy system structure and granularity required to answer them

This document is about the **model structure itself**. It is not intended to define reporting packages, scenario pipelines, QA processes, or implementation mechanics.

---

## 1. Scope and framing

The MVP is not intended to be a full replacement for every AusTIMES use case. It is intended to be the minimum credible multi-sector model needed to support AEMO’s IASR needs.

For this purpose, the model means a coordinated set of runs across scenarios and assumptions, not a single solve.

The MVP is intended to support a **multi-sector demand and emissions view of the economy**, while leaving detailed power-system modelling to AEMO unless AEMO explicitly asks otherwise.

Accordingly, the default v2 position is:

- no endogenous power-system dispatch or capacity expansion modelling
- no intra-annual load-shape modelling in the base MVP
- no charging-shape or storage-dynamics representation in the base MVP
- explicit treatment of cross-sector interactions only to the extent needed to quantify energy demand, emissions, and the implied power-sector emissions budget

---

## 2. Core questions the MVP must answer

### 2.1 Energy demand over time

The model must answer:

- What is energy demand over time across the major energy carriers under each IASR scenario?
- What causes changes in that demand over time and across scenarios?
- Which sectors, subsectors, end uses, technologies, and large industrial loads are driving those changes?

The main causes of change that the model must be able to represent are:

- demand-side activity change
- energy efficiency
- fuel switching and electrification
- technology switching
- policy settings
- cost assumptions
- hydrogen boundary assumptions
- data-centre growth
- fossil fuel export assumptions where material to energy use or emissions

### 2.2 Power-system emissions budget

The model must answer:

- What residual emissions budget or emissions envelope is implied for the power system under each IASR scenario?
- How does that budget change under different non-power-sector assumptions?
- Which assumptions and levers affect that budget the most?

This is the core reason the multi-sector model is needed: to quantify how assumptions outside the power sector affect the emissions budget that remains for the power system.

## 3. Proposed v1 granularity specification

## 3.1 Temporal granularity

| Dimension | Proposed v1 specification |
| --- | --- |
| Model years | Annual from base year to 2050 |
| Intra-annual time slices | None in the base MVP |
| Stock timing | Annual stock turnover / adoption timing where required |

## 3.2 Spatial granularity

The proposed base spatial granularity is **electricity-network level**, not state level.

| Spatial layer | Proposed v1 regions |
| --- | --- |
| Electricity-network regions | NEM, SWIS, NWIS, regulated NT system / Darwin-Katherine system, Off-grid |

All underlying data should be mapped into these regions.

The non-NEM naming above should be treated as a working proposal pending confirmation with AEMO.

## 3.3 Sector, subsector, end-use, and technology granularity

| Sector | Proposed v1 subsectors / categories | Proposed v1 end uses / services | Proposed v1 technology detail |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Buildings | Residential, Commercial | Space conditioning, water heating, cooking, other electricity | Gas, electric, heat pump where relevant; behind-the-meter supply represented explicitly if included |
| Transport | Light road, freight road, other transport aggregate | Mobility / freight service proxies and exogenous transport demand where needed | ICE, BEV, optional hydrogen freight; no charging-shape representation in base MVP |
| Industry | Named large industrial / safeguard-linked facility archetypes; rest of industry | Low/medium temperature heat, high temperature heat, non-heat electricity where needed | Generic facility or process archetypes, not bespoke site engineering models |
| Data centres | Standalone category | Electricity demand | Exogenous regional demand trajectory |
| Hydrogen supply and use | In-scope production, out-of-scope / off-grid supply, final use | Hydrogen production and final demand | Explicit distinction between in-scope and out-of-scope / off-grid supply |
| Fossil fuel exports / processing | LNG and other export-related activity where material | Energy use and emissions linkage | Treated through simplified explicit representation and/or exogenous activity assumptions |

## 3.4 Named industry detail required in v1

At minimum, the industrial narrative should be able to distinguish:

- cement
- alumina
- iron and steel
- LNG / gas processing
- other major safeguard-linked industrial loads as generic archetype groups
- the rest of industry as an aggregated residual category

## 3.5 Facility granularity principle

Where AEMO needs a facility-level narrative, the MVP should use **generic facility archetypes or facility classes**, not bespoke engineering representations of individual sites.

The purpose is to represent major transition pathways and their implications for energy use, emissions, and safeguard-related narratives, not to simulate each site in detail.

## 4. Minimum reference energy system requirements

## 4.1 Core dimensions

The reference energy system must support the following dimensions:

- year
- region
- sector
- subsector / facility class
- end use / service
- technology / process route
- energy carrier
- scenario / assumption set

## 4.2 Core carrier representation

The reference energy system must include, at minimum, the energy carriers needed to tell the IASR demand and emissions story:

- electricity
- fossil gas
- biomethane
- hydrogen
- liquid fuels
- coal, coke, and other fossil fuels where material to industrial energy use or emissions

Behind-the-meter supply should be represented explicitly where included, rather than treated only as an unexplained demand reduction.

## 4.3 Buildings representation

The model must represent buildings well enough to capture:

- gas-to-electric switching
- efficiency improvements
- the role of rooftop PV and behind-the-meter supply where included
- policy settings such as no new gas connections where relevant

## 4.4 Transport representation

The model must represent transport well enough to capture:

- light-road electrification
- freight transition
- liquid-fuels decline
- transport electricity demand growth

The base MVP does not include charging-shape representation.

## 4.5 Industry representation

The model must represent industry well enough to capture:

- switching between major energy carriers
- the distinction between large industrial / safeguard-linked loads and the rest of industry
- major process-route shifts where they matter to the narrative
- the minimum credible heat story through low/medium temperature heat and high temperature heat

## 4.6 Hydrogen boundary representation

The model must represent hydrogen explicitly enough to distinguish between:

- hydrogen produced within scope
- hydrogen supplied from out-of-scope sources
- hydrogen supplied from the off-grid region where relevant

This is needed so that hydrogen demand is physically and geographically accounted for without forcing detailed power-system modelling into the MVP.

## 4.7 Fossil fuel exports and LNG representation

The model must include a simplified explicit treatment of fossil fuel exports, especially LNG, wherever they materially affect:

- energy use
- emissions outcomes
- the residual emissions budget available to the power system

This does not require a detailed export model. It does require that export-related activity can enter the model as a driver of energy use and emissions where relevant.

## 4.8 Power-system interface

The default v1 position is that the multi-sector model does **not** endogenously represent the power system.

Instead, it should be able to operate using exogenous power-system-facing assumptions such as:

- electricity price
- electricity emissions intensity
- external power-sector budget assumptions where needed

This should remain the default unless AEMO explicitly requires more.

---

## 5. What is out of scope in the base MVP

The following are out of scope in the base MVP unless AEMO explicitly asks for them:

- endogenous power-system dispatch
- endogenous power-sector capacity expansion
- intra-annual time-slice modelling
- EV charging-shape modelling
- storage-dynamics modelling for behind-the-meter batteries
- bespoke engineering models for individual industrial facilities
- detailed reporting-schema design
- scenario-pipeline mechanics
- QA and validation framework design

## 6. Questions for AEMO

The next AEMO discussion should focus on confirming the scope and granularity of the MVP, not on detailed implementation.

### 6.1 Core purpose

1. Are the two core questions above the right ones for the MVP?
   - energy demand over time and what causes it
   - the implied power-system emissions budget and what affects it

### 6.2 Temporal granularity

2. Can the base MVP be annual only?
3. Do you need any intra-annual temporal representation in the multi-sector model at all?
4. If yes, what specific decision or output requires it?

### 6.3 Spatial granularity

5. Is electricity-network-level regionality sufficient for the MVP?
   - NEM
   - SWIS
   - NWIS
   - regulated NT / Darwin-Katherine system
   - Off-grid
6. Do any of these regions need to be merged or split differently?
7. Please confirm the exact non-NEM regional breakdown and naming you want used.

### 6.4 Power-system boundary

8. Is it acceptable for the MVP to treat the power system as exogenous, using inputs such as electricity price and electricity emissions intensity?
9. Do you require any explicit representation of the power system beyond that within the multi-sector model itself?

### 6.5 Behind-the-meter supply

10. Do you want rooftop PV and behind-the-meter batteries shown explicitly in the model structure?
11. If yes, is it acceptable for these to be treated as exogenous supply assumptions in the MVP rather than endogenous investment and dispatch decisions?

### 6.6 Industry detail

12. Is the proposed split between named large industrial / safeguard-linked loads and the rest of industry sufficient?
13. Which industrial sectors must be explicitly visible in the IASR narrative?
   - cement
   - alumina
   - iron and steel
   - LNG / gas processing
   - other safeguard sectors
14. Do you need facility classes, named facilities, or only sector-level groupings?

### 6.7 Hydrogen boundary

15. How do you want hydrogen classified between:
   - in-scope production
   - out-of-scope supply
   - off-grid supply
16. Is a separate Off-grid region sufficient to keep hydrogen accounting consistent for MVP purposes?

### 6.8 Fossil fuel exports

17. How do you want LNG and other fossil fuel export assumptions to enter the model?
   - as exogenous activity assumptions affecting energy use and emissions
   - as explicit sectoral representations within the model
   - some combination of the two
18. Do gas for power and LNG feedgas need explicit internal representation in the MVP, or can they remain exogenous where needed?

---

## 7. Bottom line

The base MVP should stay tightly focused.

It needs to answer two questions well:

1. What is the energy demand over time, and what causes it?
2. What is the implied emissions budget for the power system, and what affects it?

The proposed granularity and reference energy system structure above are intended to be the minimum needed to answer those questions credibly, while leaving detailed power-system modelling to AEMO.
